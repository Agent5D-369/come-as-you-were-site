How to talk about the project in 60 seconds

Fast opening

COME AS YOU WERE is a fictionalized premium limited series inspired by the cultural fallout around the death of a generation-defining music icon. It is not a tabloid allegation piece. It is a prestige thriller about how an official story hardens when too many people need it to.

The buy sentence

A dead legend. An inner circle with too much to lose. The mystery is not just who killed him. It is who needed the story to close — and what it cost to keep it closed.

Three points to hit on every call

Keep the conversation in the premium lane

1. Category clarity

Prestige limited series. Music-world thriller. Public-mythology mystery. Buyers should know what shelf this sits on immediately.

2. Why fictionalized

The fictionalized lane is the asset, not the compromise. It keeps the heat while improving castability, packageability, and legal survivability.

3. What you want

Packaging help, development finance, talent access, banner attachment, or companion-audio expansion. Name the lane and keep moving.

Useful lines

Talk tracks that sound expensive

When they ask “Why this now?”

Because audiences keep showing up for truth-corrupted prestige thrillers, and this one has a culturally loaded music-world hook with obvious press and companion-media upside.

When they ask “Why not use the real names?”

Because a direct allegation project is a narrower, uglier lane. The fictionalized version is broader, stronger, and much easier to package responsibly.

When they ask “What does it need?”

A serious writer-showrunner, a tone-setting director, one top-tier male lead, one awards-level female co-lead, and a banner that knows how to talk to premium buyers.

When they ask “How can I participate?”

Packaging, development financing, producer or EP attachment, rights strategy, talent access, or companion-audio/media expansion depending on fit.
