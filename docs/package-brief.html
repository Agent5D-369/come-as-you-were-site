Fast brief

COME AS YOU WERE

A premium fictionalized 6-part thriller series inspired by the cultural fallout around the death of a generation-defining music icon.

Logline

After the death of a world-famous musician is ruled closed, an outsider starts pulling at the official narrative and uncovers an inner circle distorted by addiction, money, fear, image control, and grief. The deeper he goes, the less the story becomes “who killed him?” and the more it becomes “who needed the story to close, and what did it cost to keep it closed?”

Commercial lane

Prestige limited series. Psychological thriller. Public-mythology mystery. Music-world drama. Built to attract a serious writer-showrunner, tone-setting director, castable lead roles, and a production banner that knows how to move premium material into streamer and prestige-buyer conversations.

Why buyers lean in

It solves the two biggest problems at once

It offers a familiar, instantly marketable emotional category while avoiding the weakest lane: a literal, legally sloppy accusation project. The package keeps the heat, widens the buyer pool, and leaves room for awards-level execution.

Recognizable hook

The death of an icon. A contaminated truth. Public mythology under pressure.

Castable roles

A damaged investigator, a magnetic partner, compromised insiders, and morally expensive supporting parts.

Expansion path

Companion podcast or video-audio series, soundtrack angles, press-friendly premise, and ancillary deal logic.

Format

Six episodes, each tightening the vice

  • Episode 1: The death, the scene, the first closing narrative.
  • Episode 2: Contradictory memories and the people closest to the event.
  • Episode 3: The outsider investigator becomes a problem to manage.
  • Episode 4: Money, contracts, control, addiction, and the machinery around fame.
  • Episode 5: The public story hardens into certainty.
  • Episode 6: The audience is left with the deeper corruption: the ecosystem itself.

Comparable emotional coordinates

  • Zodiac — obsessive investigation
  • Mindhunter — psychological inquiry
  • The People v. O.J. Simpson — public narrative warfare
  • Black Swan — identity, distortion, self-destruction
  • Music icon mythology — without becoming cosplay or nostalgia bait
